Irongate inks Canberra purchase; Macquarie, UBS on raising

Sarah Thompson, Anthony Macdonald and Tim Boyd
Jun 15, 2021 – 9.46am
 or Subscribe to save article

ASX-listed real estate investor Irongate Group is raising money to fund its purchase of a Canberra office building from Blackstone.

The company had Macquarie Capital and UBS in the market on Tuesday morning with a $50 million placement to partially finance the $73.75 million acquisition.

New shares in the deal were being sold to funds for $1.47 each, which was a 3.9 per cent discount to Irongate’s Friday close.

The placement was fully underwritten by Macquarie and UBS, funds were told.

The Canberra building was being bought on a 5.13 per cent yield and is 55 per cent leased to the Australian National Audit Office, which had 13.5 years left on its lease term.

The acquisition takes Irongate’s portfolio to 35 properties worth $1.33 billion.
