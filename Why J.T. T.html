Why J.T. Tuimoloau and Jack Sawyer could be Larry Johnson’s recruiting magnum opus as an Ohio State football coach: Buckeyes Recruiting

Updated 9:21 AM; Today 5:09 AM
By Stephen Means, cleveland.com

COLUMBUS, Ohio -- Larry Johnson’s legacy as Ohio State football’s defensive line coach is already set in stone.

He’s produced three top-five NFL Draft picks, five Big Ten Defensive Linemen of the Year winners and even got a kid to New York as a Heisman Trophy finalist. He doesn’t have anything to prove. But his resume could add one more bullet point to it that probably seemed unimaginable when he first got here in 2014.

The Buckeyes have one more spot left in their second-ranked 2021 recruiting class, and the player who could fill it makes his first trip to Columbus on Friday.

J.T. Tuimoloau might be Ohio State’s most intriguing recruiting target in over a decade. He’s the nation’s No. 3 player and No. 2 defensive lineman but keeps such a low profile that the only member of the media he talks to is 247Sports’ Brandon Huffman. He and his family have been diligent in how they want the recruiting process to play out and haven’t detoured from that plan.

They weren’t committing anywhere until they could take visits, and it didn’t matter how long they’d have to wait for that to happen. The dead period ended on June 1, and three days later, he took his first official visit to Washington. As you read this, he’s walking around USC’s campus and, over the next 10 days, will visit OSU, USC and Alabama.

Even though he and Johnson have never met in person, the Buckeyes have the perceived lead, making this weekend’s visit the most important of the summer. Tuimoloau will sit with arguably the best defensive line coach of all time and see why the Bosa brothers and Chase Young developed into the NFL stars they now are.

He’ll also meet Jack Sawyer, the other top-five player in the 2021 class who will undoubtedly be walking around campus this weekend. He’s been a Buckeye for six months and already has fans excited for what he could be after his performance in the Spring Game.

Johnson’s had plenty of talent come through the Woody Hayes Athletic Center, but pairing Sawyer and Tuimoloau puts him in a different stratosphere. The possibilities would be endless, and the 2023 season would provide the same hype Nick Bosa and Chase Young came into 2018 with, except Young was a first-year starter that season and still developing into the player he’d eventually be.

Fans were robbed of that experience after Bosa suffered an abdominal injury, ending his college career while Young spent the year dealing with ankle problems.

Young had the best season any of Johnson’s pupils have ever had. Expecting anyone to repeat that — both statistically and the amount of hardware he took home — wouldn’t be realistic, even at a place where the next man up is constantly raising the bar.

Johnson’s recruiting prowess is well-known. His eight cycles in Columbus have brought in five players rated as five-stars and seven others rated as top 100 recruits. The 2016 years were his most impressive when he brought in Nick Bosa and Jonathon Cooper, both ranked inside the top 35. The 2021 cycle would obviously top that, but the route there has similarities.

In both instances, one of the edge rushers was an Ohio native who grew up dreaming of Buckeyes. Cooper was that in 2016 which is why he committed two years before Signing Day in 2014. Sawyer did the same committing in 2019 — a month after Ryan Day official took over the program — and never looked back. Both fit the description of a high-rated player with a low-maintenance personality that allowed the coaching staff to focus more on national targets.

“That’s been my whole motto,” Sawyer told cleveland.com. “When I was ready to commit, that’s 100% where I was going. It was a no-brainer, obviously, and I didn’t want to really entertain anybody else.

“When you commit, you shut down. You’re not talking to other schools unless something major happens. That’s why I always kept it that way.”

Cooper taking that approach was just a bonus in 2016, given that Nick’s older brother, Joey, had just come through the program and became a star.

Tuimoloau doesn’t have the same connection, but having his high school teammate Gee Scott Jr. and fellow Washington native Emeka Egbuka already on campus can help his cause.

That 2019 season represents Johnson’s magnum opus as a developer of talent. Sawyer and Tuimoloau can represent him as a recruiter. He’d have brought in the two of the three best defensive linemen in the country in the same cycle, then spent three years developing them into top-five NFL Draft picks, only raising the ceiling of what’s possible in 2023.

Johnson’s had an impeccable coaching career spanning six decades at high school and college levels. Yet every time you think he’s reached his peak, he finds ways to outdo himself again.

This weekend we’ll see if he can do it one more time.
